Swiss Screw Machining:High-Volume Small-Diameter Parts

Swiss screw machines produce small-diameter precision parts at high volume with unmatched repeatability. Learn when to specify Swiss and what tolerances to expect.

← Back to Blog

What is Swiss Screw Machining?

Swiss screw machining (also called Swiss-type turning or Swiss automatic) is a precision turning process optimized for small-diameter, high-length-to-diameter ratio parts. Unlike conventional CNC lathes where the workpiece is held in a chuck, Swiss machines feed bar stock through a precision guide bushing. The cutting tools work immediately adjacent to the bushing, which supports the part right at the cutting zone — eliminating deflection and enabling extremely tight tolerances on long, slender parts.

The result: diameters from 0.020" to 1.25", tolerances of ±0.0002", and cycle times measured in seconds per part at production volumes.

Swiss vs. Conventional Turning

The guide bushing is the key differentiator. On a conventional lathe, a 6" long shaft with a 0.25" diameter (L/D ratio of 24:1) will deflect under cutting forces, making tight tolerances impossible without multiple passes and supports. A Swiss machine holds the same part rigid at the cut, allowing a single-pass finish cut to ±0.0002" diameter.

Use Swiss turning when:

  • Part diameter is under 1.25" (32 mm)
  • Length-to-diameter ratio exceeds 3:1
  • Tight diameter tolerances are required (±0.0005" or better)
  • Production volume is 500+ pieces (setup costs are higher)
  • Part requires multiple operations — OD turning, drilling, threading, milling — in a single setup

Common Swiss-Turned Parts

Swiss machining dominates in industries that need high volumes of small, precise components:

  • Medical: Bone screws, cannulas, catheter components, implant shafts
  • Aerospace / Defense: Connector pins, fasteners, valve stems, actuator shafts
  • Electronics: Connector contacts, standoffs, precision spacers
  • Hydraulics / Pneumatics: Valve bodies, fittings, orifice inserts
  • Semiconductor: Nozzles, probe pins, alignment features

Material Recommendations for Swiss

Brass C360 is the ideal Swiss material — free-machining, produces tight chips, and holds excellent tolerances. Default choice for connectors and fittings where conductivity or corrosion resistance is needed.

303 stainless is the best stainless choice for Swiss — the free-machining sulfur additions prevent the stringy chips that plague 304 and 316 in high-speed Swiss operations.

6061 aluminum runs fast and cheaply. Good for lightweight structural pins, spacers, and housings where corrosion isn't a concern.

Avoid gummy, work-hardening alloys (304 SS, pure titanium, Inconel) on Swiss unless required — they cause built-up edge and reduce tool life significantly.

RFQ Checklist for Swiss Parts

  1. Specify bar stock diameter range — Swiss setup is bar-size specific
  2. Call out critical diameters and their tolerances explicitly
  3. Note any live tooling features (cross-holes, flats, threads perpendicular to axis)
  4. Specify finish requirements on sealing surfaces and bearing fits
  5. Include production quantity and any annual volume estimates
  6. Note certifications required: material certs, ITAR, ISO 9001, AS9100, FDA

Swiss setup costs are front-loaded — the economics improve significantly at volumes above 1,000 pieces. Include your annual usage estimate so we can structure pricing accordingly.
