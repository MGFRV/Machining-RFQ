## When to use CNC milling

[2–3 paragraphs. Be specific: part geometries, feature types, tolerance ranges, materials.]

### Milling strengths
- Prismatic parts with pockets, bores, and complex contours
- 3-axis, 4-axis, and 5-axis capability for compound angles
- Best for: enclosures, brackets, plates, housings

## When to use CNC turning

[2–3 paragraphs.]

### Turning strengths
- Rotational parts: shafts, bushings, fittings, threaded components
- OD/ID tolerances to ±0.0005" on demand
- Best for: high-volume round parts, screw machine work

## Side-by-side comparison

| | CNC Milling | CNC Turning |
|--|--|--|
| Part geometry | Prismatic / complex | Rotational / cylindrical |
| Typical tolerance | ±0.001" | ±0.0005" |
| Setup time | Higher | Lower for high volume |
| Best volume | Prototype to mid-volume | Mid to high volume |
| Materials | All | All |
