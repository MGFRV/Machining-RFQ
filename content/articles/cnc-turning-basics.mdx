      <h2>What is CNC Turning?</h2>
      <p>CNC turning is a subtractive manufacturing process where a workpiece rotates at high speed while a stationary cutting tool removes material. The result is a cylindrical or conical part produced with high repeatability and tight tolerances.</p>
      <p>Unlike milling — where the tool moves — in turning the part moves. This fundamental difference makes turning ideal for shafts, pins, bushings, and any rotationally symmetric geometry.</p>

      <h2>When to Choose Turning Over Milling</h2>
      <p>Use turning when your part is primarily cylindrical. Common examples include:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Shafts and axles</li>
        <li>Threaded fasteners and studs</li>
        <li>Hydraulic fittings and connectors</li>
        <li>Bushings and spacers</li>
        <li>Nozzles and valve bodies</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Milling is better suited for prismatic parts with flat surfaces, pockets, or complex 3D features that can't be achieved through rotation.</p>

      <h2>Tolerances You Can Expect</h2>
      <p>Standard CNC turning tolerances run <strong>±0.005"</strong> (±0.127 mm) without special effort. With careful setup and finishing passes, tolerances of <strong>±0.001"</strong> (±0.025 mm) are achievable on diameter.</p>
      <p>Surface finish of Ra 1.6 µm (63 µin) is typical off the machine. Ground finishes can reach Ra 0.4 µm (16 µin) for bearing fits and sealing surfaces.</p>

      <h2>Material Considerations</h2>
      <p>Most machinable metals run well on a lathe. Here's a quick reference:</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>6061 Aluminum</strong> — Fast, cheap, excellent finish. Default choice for non-critical parts.</li>
        <li><strong>303 Stainless</strong> — Free-machining grade. Better choice than 304 for turned parts.</li>
        <li><strong>4140 Steel</strong> — Strong, tough, machines well. Good for shafts under load.</li>
        <li><strong>Brass (C360)</strong> — Exceptional machinability. Great for fittings and connectors.</li>
        <li><strong>Titanium Grade 5</strong> — Slow feeds, sharp tools, coolant required. High cost but high performance.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Specifying Your Turned Parts</h2>
      <p>When submitting an RFQ for turned parts, include:</p>
      <ol>
        <li>STEP or IGES file (or a 2D print with full GD&amp;T)</li>
        <li>Material and any required certifications (e.g., material certs, ITAR)</li>
        <li>Critical tolerances called out explicitly — don't rely on title block defaults for anything that matters</li>
        <li>Surface finish requirements and any post-processing (anodize, plating, passivation)</li>
        <li>Quantity and target delivery</li>
      </ol>
      <p>The more complete your package, the faster and more accurate the quotes you'll receive.</p>
