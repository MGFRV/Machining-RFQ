## The short answer

Material certification terms sound similar and often are not. Buyers need to separate supplier statements from mill-origin evidence and from full traceability packages.

## Cert of conformance versus mill evidence

A certificate of conformance is usually the supplier's statement that the delivered material or part meets the specified requirement. An MTR or mill cert generally ties chemistry and mechanical data back to a heat or lot from the mill. Those are not the same level of evidence.

Both can be valid. They simply answer different questions.

## Where chemical analysis fits

Chemical analysis data matters when alloy identity, customer flowdown, or regulatory expectation requires proof of chemistry. Sometimes the mill cert is enough. Sometimes independent or additional verification is required by the customer or risk level.

Do not assume the same package is necessary for every part.

## Traceability level matters

A cert is not full traceability unless the chain from raw material to finished part is preserved and documented. That may mean lot segregation, traveler control, or serialized association depending on the program. High-risk sectors often require that discipline.

Prototype work often does not.

## Write the requirement cleanly

State whether you need a supplier cert, mill cert, traceable MTR, chemical analysis, or full lot traceability to shipped parts. Ambiguous 'material certs required' language is why receiving inspection ends up in arguments.

Say what evidence you need, not just that you want paperwork.

## Comparison table where relevant

| Document type | What it proves | Typical use |
| -- | -- | -- |
| Certificate of conformance | Supplier states compliance | General purchase confirmation |
| MTR / mill cert | Mill-linked chemistry and properties | Critical raw material verification |
| Chemical analysis report | Specific chemistry evidence | Higher-control programs |
| Full traceability package | Chain from raw to finished part | Aerospace, defense, regulated work |


## How to specify this in your RFQ

List the exact material documentation you need in the RFQ and PO. If traceability to each shipped lot is required, say how it must be identified. Do not expect the supplier to guess your end-customer paperwork standard.

A clean RFQ does not just list requirements. It separates must-haves from preferences so the supplier can optimize where it is safe.
